About NASA

NASA, the National Aeronautics and Space Administration, is the United States government agency responsible for the nation's civilian space program and for aeronautics and aerospace research

About the NASA

  • Established: July 29, 1958
  • Headquarters: Washington, D.C.
  • Administrator: The head of NASA, currently Bill Nelson (as of 2021).
  • Motto: "For the Benefit of All

Mission

  • Exploration of Space: NASA conducts manned and unmanned space missions to explore celestial bodies, including the Moon, Mars, and beyond.
  • Scientific Research: The agency conducts research to understand Earth, the solar system, and the universe, including the effects of climate change and other phenomena.
  • Aeronautics: NASA develops technologies to improve air travel, enhance safety, and reduce environmental impact.
